Over half the world's population lives under the threat of Malaria

Each year, hundreds of thousands die from this deadly, yet preventable illness.

WHAT IS IT?

Malaria is known as a vector-borne illness. The infected mosquito's saliva carries a parasite of the genus Plasmodium, which enters the bloodstream by a mosquito bite.

If infected, initial symptoms will appear 7-15 days after the bite, and include:
  • fever
  • headache
  • chills
  • vomiting

Malaria can infect anyone, however children and pregnant women are the most susceptible to the lethal concerns of the disease.

WHERE DOES IT OCCUR?

Malaria is endemic to 97 countries worldwide.

Generally in the tropical and subtropic regions of the world, where mosquitoes can thrive.

But 90% of deaths occur in sub-Saharan Africa.

Why? Areas in Africa are the most affected due to a combination of factors:
  • The warm weather conditions allow for transmission to occur year-round
  • Scarce resources and socio-economic instability have hindered efficient malaria control activities.
  • The dominant parasite species in the area is Plasmodium falciparum, which is the species that is most likely to cause severe malaria.

PREVENTION

Malaria is curable, but treatment needs to be prompt and accessible, which is a problem in some remote areas of Africa. In this case, malaria PREVENTION is key in fighting the global threat of malaria.
